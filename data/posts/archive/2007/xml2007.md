I have not been there myself, but as we heard from those who attended XML 2007, there were quite a few talks mentioning eXist: Erik Bruchez: [XForms and the eXist XML database: a perfect couple](http://2007.xmlconference.org/public/schedule/detail/426) Dan McCreary: [Using XForms and eXist to Manage Metadata](http://2007.xmlconference.org/public/schedule/detail/425) Kurt Cagle: [Lightweight XML](http://2007.xmlconference.org/public/schedule/detail/256) Mark Birbeck: [XForms, XHTML, and RDFa for Internet-Facing Applications](http://2007.xmlconference.org/public/schedule/detail/427) Mark Birbeck: [XForms on the Desktop using Sidewinder](http://2007.xmlconference.org/public/schedule/detail/400) The slides for the first two talks are available online. We also heard Norm Walsh mentioned an [XProc implementation in XQuery](http://2007.xmlconference.org/public/schedule/detail/278) based on eXist (I guess this must be Jim Fuller's [XProcXQ](http://code.google.com/p/xprocxq/)). XProc is an interesting new standard and I would love to see a simple implementation which can be easily integrated with eXist.
